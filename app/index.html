Stephanie Munch Jensen

Being a developer- and designer-hybrid I like to know a bit about everything. I am eager to learn and thrive on challenges forcing me to expand my skillset. When I get to work on things I’m good at, I become very passionate and tend to leave out everything else until I’m satisfied with the outcome. I pay attention to details and put a great amount of honor in optimizing and tweaking all the little things that in total makes an even better and faster product. Contrary to my profession I enjoy being active and pushing myself and my body towards new goals. I believe that the only way to be truly happy is to continuously accomplish and set new goals - both personally and professionally.

Birth

First programming experience
During my last year at HTX i had Programming as a subject which introduced me to Visual Basic and my passion for programming was then discovered.

With my exam from the Higher Technical Examination Programme (HTX) I was ready to go to the big city and start following my dreams within web development. 3 months later I started studying Multimediadesign and Communication at Copenhagen School of Technology and Design (KEA).

Moved to CPH
After spending three months commuting 4 hours a day to my school in Copenhagen I decided it was time to make the move and live where all the fun things were happening.

First job: Webdeveloper, Better Collective
While doing my studies at KEA I felt like getting some more practical experience so I joined Better Collective for a webdeveloper-position where I spent 4 years working with PHP, MySQL, Javscript, HTML/CSS, and UX-design on various inhouse websites related to the iGaming-industry (Sports-betting and Casino).
June
Multimediadesigner-graduate
I graduated as a multimediadesigner in the summer of 2011 and felt I had a solid base for continuing my career within webdevelopment, so I applied for the Webdeveloper BA-programme – also at KEA – where I then studied while working fulltime at Better Collective.
September
Appartment-owner
At this point I felt things were running pretty well; I was almost a webdeveloper-graduate, I had a nice fulltime job as a webdeveloper, and I LOVED Copenhagen. So I decided to make the joy more permanent by buying my first apartment and moving closer to the city.
January
Webdeveloper-graduate
This is the time where I could finally say I had a proper education – although most of my knowledge was gained through my job. I finished my education by doing a 6-month project for my employer, building a website management system for managing user-access and FTP-credentials for their huge list of domains. The system was based on the MVC-concept and built with PHP, MySQL, HTML/CSS, and Javascript.
February
Volunteer: Nørrebro Fighters
In my sparetime I like to exercise and keep myself fit so when I got the opportunity to join a newly founded Kickboxing-club and help them get successful I immediately said yes. After that I spent the next 2 years being in charge of their website, creating promotional material and function as accountant.
November
Joined Citrix
After four years at Better Collective I decided it was time for change. I had gained a bigger interest in frontend-development and wanted to work on a singlepage-application project where I could really gain a lot of new knowledge. So I joined Citrix together with a higly skilled new team and started working on the next generation of their GoToAssist product-line.
